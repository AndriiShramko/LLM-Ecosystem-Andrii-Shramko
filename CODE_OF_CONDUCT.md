# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want a **warm, curious, and respectful community** building something weird and wonderful together. We pledge to make participation a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that helps:

- Being kind and assuming good faith.
- Crediting others' work (and respecting the project's attribution requirement to Andrii Shramko).
- Welcoming newcomers and helping them get oriented.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Project-specific non-negotiables

Because of what this project *is*, these are part of the code of conduct:

- **Sandbox-safety always.** No real-world actuators, no network side effects, energy stays zero-sum and offline. Do not try to route around the safety gates.
- **Honest anthropomorphism — no dark patterns.** Always disclose that the "creatures" are AI models. The aquarium metaphor is for teaching and engagement, never to deceive or manipulate.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the lead maintainer at **zmei116@gmail.com**. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions, comments, and contributors that violate this Code of Conduct.

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), version 2.1.
